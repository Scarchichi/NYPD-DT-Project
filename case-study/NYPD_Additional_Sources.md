## Targeted Secondary Sources (Tier 2 and Tier 3)
These are the sources we should prioritize collecting to strengthen the case narrative and verification.

### A. Civil liberties and oversight
1. NYCLU / ACLU reports on DAS and NYPD surveillance
   - expected content: privacy risks, contracts, governance criticisms
2. Civil rights testimony from City Council hearings or public forums
   - expected content: community concerns, oversight demands, policy recommendations

### B. Investigative reporting
1. ProPublica coverage of license plate readers and national tracking systems
   - expected content: history of LPR use, data retention practices, vendor relationships
2. The Intercept or other investigative journalism on camera search and race
   - expected content: alleged racial profiling, camera search capabilities, civil liberties analysis
3. New York Times coverage of NYPD facial recognition and DAS-related privacy issues
   - expected content: public debate, NYPD response, technology details

### C. Academic and policy research
1. RAND, Harvard, or peer-reviewed articles on urban surveillance or smart policing
   - expected content: analytic bias, surveillance governance, technology efficacy
2. Research on Patternizr or similar predictive/analytical policing tools
   - expected content: algorithmic design, bias concerns, evaluation studies

### D. Funding and operational context
1. Federal grant documentation or DHS funding announcements tied to DAS
   - expected content: funding amounts, program objectives, federal oversight
2. NYPD budget and counterterrorism expenditure statements
   - expected content: allocation of resources, operational priorities, political tradeoffs

## Source Verification and Quality
Use the following criteria when adding sources:
- **Tier 1**: Full-text primary sources available locally in `sources/`.
- **Tier 2**: Partial or paywalled sources where key passages are captured.
- **Tier 3**: Referenced sources not yet downloaded or verified.

Maintain a separate entry for each source in `sources/Source_Registry.md`.

## Research Notes
- Balance the NYPD’s operational claims with independent scrutiny.
- Prioritize quotes from named decision-makers, city officials, and advocacy leaders.
- Capture specific incidents or examples of DAS use, especially where privacy and policing intersect.
- Document the governance framework and the criticism of that framework clearly.
- Keep the case grounded in publicly verifiable facts and explicit citations.
